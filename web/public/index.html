How Dragonfly works

A simple rhythm for noticing the living world.

1

Go outside

Kids take a photo of something living: a plant, insect, bird, mushroom, or other organism.

2

Log the observation

Dragonfly helps them record what they found. They can choose a suggested species or enter what they know.

3

See what changed

Each observation fills their Dex, advances expeditions, and brings new life to their Sanctuary.

Meet The Sanctuary

A world that only grows from real discoveries.

The Sanctuary is a quiet illustrated habitat inside Dragonfly. It starts sparse. As kids log real observations, the meadow grows, the pond ripples, birds visit the sky, and tiny details appear.

  • Your first plant wakes up the meadow.
  • A butterfly brings movement to the flowers.
  • A frog adds ripples to the pond.
  • More observations make the scene richer over time.

For adults

Families and teachers

Made for invited groups, not a public social network.

Dragonfly is designed for invited families and classrooms. Parents and teachers create kid accounts and groups. Kids explore inside a supervised, closed group.

FAQ

Clear answers for the first pilot families.

What age is Dragonfly for?

Dragonfly is designed for curious explorers of all ages. Kid accounts are managed by adults.

Is Dragonfly public?

No. Dragonfly is preparing for a limited Android Internal Testing pilot with invited families and adult supervision.

Is Dragonfly a public social network?

No. Dragonfly has no public kid profiles, public chat, direct messages, follower counts, or open discovery.

Does Dragonfly have ads?

No. Dragonfly does not include ads, tracking pixels, ad SDKs, or marketing chat widgets.

What data does Dragonfly collect?

During the pilot, Dragonfly may collect organism photos, observation location, timestamp, species or taxon choices, kid nickname or display name, group membership, and adult account contact details. The privacy page explains the pilot draft in more detail.

What is The Sanctuary?

The Sanctuary is a private illustrated habitat that grows from real observations. It is not a public profile, social feed, or map.

Can teachers use Dragonfly?

Teacher and group-leader use is planned for controlled pilot groups, nature clubs, school gardens, and field walks. Teacher access stays invite-only during testing.

Is Dragonfly available now?

No. Dragonfly is preparing for a closed Android pilot. Broader release depends on privacy, legal, store-review, and field-testing gates.

Is this available on iPhone?

Not yet. The first pilot is focused on Android through Google Play Internal Testing.

How do I request pilot access?

Use the pilot request button or email support@dragonfly-app.net. Please do not include your child’s full name in this request.

How do I get support?

Email support@dragonfly-app.net or visit the support page. For privacy questions, use privacy@dragonfly-app.net.
