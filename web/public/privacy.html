Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-12 (draft)

What Dragonfly is

Dragonfly is a citizen-science field app for kids ages 9–12. Kids photograph plants and animals they find outdoors. Each photo plus the location it was taken becomes a scientific observation that contributes to iNaturalist, a global species database used by researchers.

A parent or teacher creates the kid’s account. The kid never types an email address, never sees ads, and never communicates with anyone outside their family or classroom group.

What we collect from a kid account

  • A photo of an organism (plant, animal, fungus, etc.). Stored encrypted at rest in Google Cloud Storage. Photos that pass automated moderation are retained as part of the observation record. Photos that fail moderation (Cloud Vision SafeSearch flags for adult, violence, racy, or medical content) are quarantined and reviewed by an adult before being approved or deleted.
  • The location the photo was taken, captured from the device at the moment of capture. The full coordinates are stored privately; only a coarse 4-character geohash (roughly a 30×30 km cell) is shared outside the kid’s group.
  • A display name chosen by the adult who created the account. We recommend a first name or a nickname; we never ask for a last name.
  • An age band (9–10, 11–12, or 13+). We do not collect the exact birth date.
  • A species identification for each photo, derived from the iNaturalist computer-vision API.

What we do not collect from a kid account

  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Last name
  • Exact date of birth
  • Anything the kid types beyond an optional caption on a photo
  • Advertising identifiers
  • Contacts, microphone, or behavioral analytics

What we collect from a parent or teacher account

  • Email address (used for sign-in via Firebase Authentication)
  • Display name
  • The groups they own and the kid accounts they have provisioned

Where data is stored

All Dragonfly data lives in Google Cloud (Cloud SQL Postgres for structured data, Cloud Storage for photos). Authentication is managed by Firebase. Data is encrypted at rest. The Google Cloud project is in the United States (us-central1).

Sharing with iNaturalist

When an adult in the kid’s group approves an observation, the photo, the coarse location, and the species identification are uploaded to iNaturalist via their public API. No personally identifying information about the child is included. The observation appears in iNaturalist under a Dragonfly-owned service account, not under the kid’s name.

Parental rights (COPPA)

A parent or teacher who created a kid account can, at any time, request:

  • A copy of the data associated with the kid account
  • Deletion of the kid account and all associated data
  • Recall of any observation already shared with iNaturalist

Email privacy@dragonfly-app.net with the request and the kid’s display name. We will respond within 14 days.
